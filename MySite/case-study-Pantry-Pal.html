Pantry Pal Mobile App Design

We discovered that people don't like to waste food, and they sometimes lose track of what they have & need. We believe that there is an opportunity to help our users upgrade their paper grocery lists into a digitized version that gives detailed information on items that they buy.

Research

To start off, we created a user interview consisting of 17 questions. We interviewed 5 users and were focusing on 2 key objectives. The below shows the objectives and some key quotes from each:

Objective 1 - Understand the grocery planning process

“I tend to shop as needed. I keep a list. I [go] once or twice or twice a week, I guess. Just to pick up fresh. I pick up meats and those things that keep longer and don't buy that every time because I'm able to buy some and then freeze some.”

“I try to use what's left in my fridge or pantry first. And then if I don't have anything then I just try to find a really cool recipe”

Objective 2 - Understand the user’s approach to food organization

“So I [try] to keep the open items in front of on top of the closed items. Sometimes that even doesn't work.”

“The berries mold at times. I'm sad to see that.”

Next, we put out a survey which yielded 45 responses. The two most insightful responses were the following:

1. What type of grocery prep do you do?

2. Do you use anything to help you grocery plan?

Definition & Ideation

Feature Prioritization

  • Create Shopping List
  • Expiration Date Alerts
  • Shareable app/list with Household
  • Live update/alert when something is added by household

Journey Map

  1. Notification - Milk Expired!
  2. Did not purchase with weekly groceries
  3. PantryPal can automatically add to new list
  4. Husband adds additional item to list while at store
  5. Able to grab both items before check out
  6. Still gets home on time with ingredients for dinner
  7. PantryPal’s features saved the evening!


Storyboard

1. Milk is expired, oh no!

At a meeting with a client, Britney gets a notification that her milk is expired & has the horrible realization that she forgot to buy milk during her weekly trip!

2. PantryPal to the rescue!

PantryPal asks if she'd like to add Milk to her grocery list, and Britney accepts.

3. Ready to go.

She gets to the store to grab the milk and is ready to check out.

4. A surprise item is added!

She gets a notification that her husband also added butter to the list and is relieved she can grab it before she checked out.

5. Home on time!

Britney is able to get home with dinner on the table before 7:00.

6. PantryPal is a success!

The kids get to bed on time and Britney is thrilled that PantryPal saved their evening!
